# Regulated, MedTech & Digital Health

For product leaders building software and devices in regulated healthcare. Regulation is
a design constraint and a source of moat — treat it as part of product strategy, not a
gate at the end.

> Regulatory guidance changes frequently. Always confirm current requirements against the
> primary source (FDA, IMDRF) and your regulatory/quality partners before acting.

## Core regulatory references (FDA)

- **[Software as a Medical Device (SaMD)](https://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/digital-health-center-excellence/software-medical-device-samd)** — risk-based classification and the FDA's SaMD program.
- **[Digital Health Center of Excellence](https://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/digital-health-center-excellence)** — FDA's hub for digital health policy and resources.
- **[Medical Device Software Guidance Navigator](https://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/regulatory-accelerator/medical-device-software-guidance-navigator)** — find the guidances relevant to a given product.
- **[Good Machine Learning Practice (GMLP) Guiding Principles](https://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/software-medical-device-samd/good-machine-learning-practice-medical-device-development-guiding-principles)** — joint FDA / Health Canada / MHRA principles; essential for ML-enabled devices.
- **Predetermined Change Control Plans (PCCP)** — how to plan and authorize iterative changes to ML models post-market.
- **Clinical Decision Support (CDS) software guidance** — when CDS is or isn't a regulated device (updated guidance, 2026).

## International & standards

- **[IMDRF](https://www.imdrf.org)** — international SaMD definitions, risk categorization, and clinical evaluation frameworks.
- **IEC 62304** (software lifecycle), **ISO 14971** (risk management), **ISO 13485** (QMS), **EU MDR** — the standards your quality system will be built around.

## Pathways to know

- **510(k)** (substantial equivalence), **De Novo** (novel low/moderate risk), **PMA** (high risk), and **Breakthrough Device** designation. Pathway choice shapes timeline, evidence, and roadmap.

## Industry, community & market intelligence

- **[The MedTech Conference (AdvaMed)](https://themedtechconference.com)** — the leading global medtech event; 2026 in Boston, Oct 18–21.
- **[AdvaMed](https://www.advamed.org)** — the medtech industry association; policy and digital health advocacy.
- **[RAPS](https://www.raps.org)** — Regulatory Affairs Professionals Society; training, certification (RAC), and events.
- **[Rock Health](https://rockhealth.com)** — digital health funding reports and market research.
- **[Greenlight Guru](https://www.greenlight.guru/blog)** — practical medical-device QMS and SaMD content.
- **[HIMSS](https://www.himss.org)** — health IT and interoperability community and conference.
- **J.P. Morgan Healthcare Conference** — the industry's annual strategy and investment bellwether.

## Interoperability & clinical workflow

- **HL7 FHIR**, **DICOM**, **EHR integration**, and **SMART on FHIR** — the standards that determine whether a clinical product fits real workflows.
