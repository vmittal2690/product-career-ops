# AI Product Management

Building AI/ML-powered products adds data strategy, model evaluation, continuous
monitoring, and human-AI interaction design to the PM's job. The product fundamentals
still apply — the failure modes are new.

## Foundations

- **[DeepLearning.AI — AI for Everyone](https://www.deeplearning.ai/courses/ai-for-everyone/)** (Andrew Ng) — non-technical mental model of what ML can and cannot do.
- **[IBM AI Product Manager Professional Certificate](https://www.coursera.org/professional-certificates/ibm-ai-product-manager)** (Coursera) — applied AI PM skills.
- **Designing Machine Learning Systems** & **AI Engineering** — Chip Huyen. How production ML/LLM systems actually work, for PMs partnering with ML and platform teams.
- **The AI Product Playbook** — Oji & Ezinne Udezue.

## Human-AI interaction & responsible AI

- **[Google People + AI Guidebook (PAIR)](https://pair.withgoogle.com/guidebook/)** — patterns for designing human-centered AI features.
- **[Microsoft HAX Toolkit](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/haxtoolkit/)** — the 18 Guidelines for Human-AI Interaction, with patterns and a design library.
- **NIST AI Risk Management Framework** — a structured way to reason about AI risk and trustworthiness.

## What changes for the PM

- **Data is the product surface** — sourcing, labeling, rights, drift, and feedback loops are roadmap items, not afterthoughts.
- **Evaluation is continuous** — define offline and online quality metrics; ship behind evals, not just unit tests.
- **Uncertainty is a UX problem** — design for confidence, error recovery, explainability, and graceful failure.
- **Monitoring never ends** — model performance degrades; plan for drift detection, retraining, and rollback.
- **Cost and latency are features** — model choice, caching, and routing are product decisions with real tradeoffs.
